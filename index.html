Here's why Annie is the right Braille-learning device for your child

Typing

  • Universal design based on the ubiquitous Perkins Brailler.
  • Soft keys that are easy to press.

Writing

  • A one-of-its-kind digital slate that mimics writing on paper.
  • Softer to press compared to paper

Reading

  • Two large cells introduce the child to Braille.
  • 6 standard-size Braille cells work in conjunction with the typing and writing modules for simultaneous learning.

Audio feedback

  • Annie talks to the child and guides them theough the lessons.
  • Inbuilt speakers and earphone jack provide multiple sources of sound.

Child-friendly

  • Super Light-weight. Weighs 400 grams.
  • Only child-friendly educational content.

Data tracking

  • Monitor usage of the child.
  • Track learning progress and outcomes.

How will Annie change the way Braille is learnt?

At home

Independent learning

In-built lessons help the child learn Braille without a teacher.

Interactive learning

Games and interactive exercises keep the child engaged while learning.

Homework

Digital Braille homework delivery and effortless evaluation.

Instant feedback

The child need not wait long for the teacher to correct a mistake.

At school

Connected teaching

The child connects to the teacher's Annie and follows the same lesson.

Continuous engagement

Has the child finshed a lesson? Easily move on to the next one.

Progress tracking

Teacher monitors the child usage and tracks progress towards learning outcomes.

Instant feedback

The child need not wait hours for someone to correct a mistake.
